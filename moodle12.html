MAHATMA GANDHI

Mahatma Gandhi, byname of Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi,
(born October 2, 1869, Porbandar, India—died January 30, 1948, Delhi)

  1. Indian lawyer, politician, social activist, and writer who became
    the leader of the nationalist movement against the British rule of India.
  2. As such, he came to be considered the father of his country.
  3. Gandhi is internationally esteemed for his doctrine of nonviolent
    rotest (satyagraha) to achieve political and social progress.
  4. In the eyes of millions of his fellow Indians,
    Gandhi was the Mahatma (“Great Soul”).
  5. The unthinking adoration of the huge crowds that gathered to
    see him all along the route of his tours made them a severe ordeal;
    he could hardly work during the day or rest at night.
